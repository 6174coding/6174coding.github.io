Trigonometry

The really simple first steps

So you've probably heard of the three main trigonometric functions, right? You remember what they are? Sine, cosine, and tangent. But you probably don't know what they actually mean. Let me make things clear on this web page. Let's say we have a random right triangle, which is a triangle with one right angle. The longest side of the triangle is called the hypotenuse. Sine is the ratio (at one angle) between the angle's opposite side and the hypotenuse. Cosine is the ratio between the angle's adjacent side (not the hypotenuse) and the hypotenuse. And finally, tangent is the ratio between the opposite side and adjacent one. You can remember all of this using the following mnemonic: SOH CAH TOA.

Next, I will teach you some easy trigonometric properties that will just aid you with trigonometry in the future. Don't worry if you don't know what they mean, because in the future, that will all be explained. (sin^2)x + (cos^2)x = 1, and this set of 4: sin(A + B) = sinAcosB + cosAsinB, sin(A - B) = sinAcosB - cosAsinB, cos(A + B) = cosAcosB - sinAsinB, and cos(A - B) = cosAcosB + sinAsinB.
